## Fan types and their best uses

Different fan types suit different situations:

- **Tower fans** are slim, oscillate well, and suit living rooms and bedrooms.
- **Desk fans** work for personal cooling at workstations.
- **Portable handheld fans** are for outdoor use and commuting.
- **Stand fans** cover larger areas but take more floor space.

Choose based on where you need cooling most.

## Key features for cooling

Not all fans cool equally. Look for:

- **Oscillation:** Side-to-side movement spreads air across a wider area.
- **Speed settings:** Multiple speeds let you adjust for comfort and noise.
- **Airflow power:** Stronger motors move more air, but also use more electricity.
- **Timer function:** Auto shut-off saves energy when you fall asleep.

For bedrooms, quiet operation matters as much as cooling power.

## Noise levels matter

Fans can disrupt sleep or concentration if too loud. Consider:

- **Tower fans** are generally quieter than stand fans.
- **Lower speed settings** reduce noise but also airflow.
- **Bladeless designs** tend to be quieter but cost more.

Check reviews for noise feedback, especially if using in bedrooms.

## Energy efficiency

Fans use far less electricity than air conditioners:

- A typical tower fan uses 50-60 watts.
- A portable fan uses 5-15 watts.
- Running a fan 8 hours daily costs a fraction of AC costs.

For budget households, fans can significantly reduce monthly electric bills during summer.

## Cooling tips for Philippine weather

- **Use fans with AC strategically:** A fan helps circulate cool air, letting you set AC higher and save electricity.
- **Position matters:** Point fans toward where you spend time, not just at the center of the room.
- **Night cooling:** If outdoor air is cooler at night, use a fan near a window to pull in fresh air.
- **Combine with ice:** A bowl of ice in front of a fan creates a DIY air cooler effect.

## Quick FAQ

- **Question:** Are tower fans better than stand fans?

  **Answer:** Tower fans are quieter and take less space. Stand fans typically move more air at lower cost. Choose based on room size and noise tolerance.

- **Question:** How long do portable fan batteries last?

  **Answer:** Most 3000-4000mAh fans last 2-8 hours depending on speed setting. Lower speeds extend battery life significantly.

- **Question:** Can a fan replace air conditioning?

  **Answer:** Fans cannot lower room temperature like AC, but they make you feel cooler through air movement. For extreme heat, AC may still be necessary.
