## Essential cleaning tools

Every home needs basics:

- **Broom and dustpan:** For daily dry sweeping.
- **Mop:** For wet cleaning hard floors.
- **Microfiber cloths:** For dusting and wiping surfaces.
- **Scrub brush:** For tough stains and bathroom cleaning.
- **Squeegee:** For windows and mirrors.

Start with these before buying specialized equipment.

## Match tools to floor types

Different floors need different care:

- **Tiles:** Mops work well; avoid excess water in grout lines.
- **Vinyl and linoleum:** Damp mop with mild cleaners.
- **Wood and laminate:** Barely damp mop; excess water causes damage.
- **Cement:** Can handle wet mopping and scrubbing.

Using the wrong technique can damage floors or leave residue.

## Mop types compared

Several mop styles are available:

- **Traditional mop and bucket:** Affordable, effective, but requires wringing.
- **Spin mop:** Easier wringing with spinning mechanism.
- **Flat mop with spray:** Convenient, less water, good for quick cleaning.
- **Steam mop:** Sanitizes without chemicals, but costs more.

For most small homes, a spin mop or flat spray mop offers the best convenience-to-price ratio.

## Storage in small spaces

Cleaning tools can clutter small homes:

- **Wall-mounted holders:** Keep brooms and mops off the floor.
- **Compact designs:** Flat mops store easier than traditional mops.
- **Multi-purpose tools:** Fewer tools mean less storage needed.
- **Collapsible buckets:** Save space when not in use.

Choose tools that fit your available storage space.

## Cleaning tips for small homes

- **Clean more often:** Small spaces show dirt faster but are quicker to clean.
- **Top to bottom:** Dust high surfaces first, then sweep and mop floors.
- **Declutter first:** Less stuff means easier cleaning.
- **Ventilate:** Open windows when cleaning to dry floors faster and reduce chemical fumes.
- **Maintenance matters:** Rinse mop heads and wash cloths after use.

## Quick FAQ

- **Question:** How often should I replace mop heads?

  **Answer:** Every 2-3 months for frequent use, or when they look worn or smell despite washing.

- **Question:** Are microfiber cloths really better?

  **Answer:** Yes. Microfiber traps dust and dirt more effectively than cotton, and works well with less water or cleaning solution.

- **Question:** Should I buy a robot vacuum?

  **Answer:** Robot vacuums work well for daily maintenance but cannot replace deep cleaning. They are useful if you can afford one, but not essential.
